01 / Raytheon Drone Competition

Fall 2023- Spring 2024

Senior Capstone with focused on the Unmanned Ground Vehicle ⏤Working in a multidisciplinary team to compete in a series of challenges, developing an Unmanned Ground Vehicle and an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle.

 

The UGV was designed to remotely complete preplanned routes with precise turning, route autocorrection, and alert upon an opponent UAV attack.

Tech Stack

ROS 2, Python, Shell Scripting

 

 

Raspberry Pi 4B, BNO055 IMU Sensor, Water Sensor

Hardware Design

 

Four DC motors are connected to two H-Bridges to regulate speed and voltage of the motors.

 

H-Bridges connect to the Raspberry Pi 4 to communicate between Pi and DC motors.

 

IMU allows the software to complete and autocorrect routes with less than 10% deviation from start point.

 

Onboard UGV water sensor detects strikes from opponent UAV. Lights and speakers alert when struck.

Software Design

 

UGV was designed with remote start on a ROS based system.

 

Robot Controller sent instructions about the preplanned route to the DOF node (connected to the IMU sensor) and the Motor Controller.

 

Water sensor node is continuously checking for an opponent strike and sends designated commands upon strike.

Field testing and calibration at competition!
